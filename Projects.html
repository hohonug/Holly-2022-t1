Accessibility and Understanding

It is integral to my creative process and general ethos that whatever I am working with is as accessible as possible. I have been trained to understand the value of accessible design and representation.

With each project that I have worked on, before I start writing, I interview people and follow their perspective as well as research whatever topic or idea I am running with. It is important to me to know more than my own opinion and so even if it is for a short story I will strategically question relevant people and consider different options to how something can be viewed. It is a matter of something new turning up that I had not considered before that can really build or stop a story.

In the cases of covering characters, people, or stories that might be out of my own understanding, I have found that it is always best to ask those who might be affected by the content or know someone who could be. In this way, I can ensure the experience of all involved will be more likely to be positive.
